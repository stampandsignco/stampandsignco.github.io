Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Remote Online Notarization is a legally authorized process that allows a notary public and a signer to complete a notarization via a secure, encrypted video session. Both parties are present in real time — the notary verifies your identity, witnesses your signature, and applies their digital seal. The result carries the same legal weight as traditional in-person notarization.

All you need is a smartphone, tablet, or computer with a working camera and microphone, a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport), and the document you need notarized. No software downloads are required — we use a secure browser-based platform.

Yes. Remote Online Notarization is fully legal and authorized in Florida. Notarized documents completed via RON carry the same legal validity as traditionally notarized documents and are accepted by courts, financial institutions, government agencies, and most other entities. If you have a specific document or jurisdiction question, contact us to confirm acceptance before booking.

Same-day appointments are often available, including evenings and weekends. We understand that notarization needs can be urgent — contact us directly and we'll do our best to accommodate your timeline.

Most documents that can be notarized in person can also be notarized remotely, including powers of attorney, affidavits, wills and trusts, healthcare directives, real estate documents, business agreements, and more. Some documents — such as certain I-9 employment verifications — may require in-person notarization by law. Contact us if you're unsure about your specific document.

Pricing varies depending on the number of documents and signatures required. Contact us for a quick, no-obligation quote — most sessions are straightforward and priced fairly. There are no hidden fees or travel charges.
